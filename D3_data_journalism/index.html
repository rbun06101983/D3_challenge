Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Examining the data collected from the 2014 ACS 1-year estimates, we could analyze a variety of different analyses about health risk. In the chart above, a comparison was made between poverty and healthcare. As one could see, the states with the higher percentage of poverty also will have the higher percentage of those who lack healthcare. There could be other factors that contribute to this correlation but the chart shows good evidence regarding the correlation bewtween poverty and healthcare.

Hovering over each point will correspond to a state that will show the exact percentage of the poulation that are in poverty and lacks healthcare

Other data could give many insights. Other correlations that could be made are between poverty, healthcare, age, household income, obesity, etc.
